Notes

Paul, Weiss alumni are successful in every field, and we are proud to highlight their accomplishments. To share professional updates for publication, please contact Sarah Marchitto, Senior Alumni Manager, at smarchitto@paulweiss.com.

For in-depth features on firm alumni and history, visit the alumni section of the Paul, Weiss website.

Notes from the 2024 March Alumni Bulletin

  • DON WALLACE (1957–1958; 1960–1962) continues to serve as chairman of the International Law Institute and as a Georgetown law professor. He always thinks fondly of the firm.
  • DUNCAN CAMERON (1959–1962) has retired from the practice of law, and occasionally provides legal advice. He values the time that he spent at Paul, Weiss and the good training that he received. Duncan still lives in Washington and vacations every summer in the Adirondacks.
  • BERNIE FILLER (1962–1969) became executive vice president and a director of Storm Drilling and Marine following his tenure at Paul, Weiss. After Storm Drilling’s acquisition by a larger competitor, Bernie was briefly a partner of a Chicago-based law firm where he headed the corporate department. Then, he served as chief executive officer and managing general partner for nine years over 23 private investment partnerships. Today, Bernie is an independent investment banker for his Chicago-based firm, Lidemara Enterprises LLC. A distinguishing aspect of his professional career is that for the past 12 years, he has traveled to China several times and has been active in creating cross-border relationships between China and North America. Bernie has three children and eight grandchildren, as well as three great grandchildren who were born in the past five months.
  • JEREMIAH GINSBERG (Entertainment, 1963–1964) is a musical writer and producer whose very own musical Mendel’s Messiah is in pre-production for a motion picture. He composed and orchestrated the music, wrote the lyrics, co-wrote the book and produced the show with his beautiful wife, Wendy, through their company, Jeremiah Theatricals. The show won a Bronze Halo Award from The Southern California Motion Picture Council in 1981, was produced off-Broadway at The Perry Street Theatre in 1985 and, most recently, was produced in a concert version production in Washington, D.C. in 2022. They are excited to be preparing a screenplay for the motion picture.
  • STEPHEN GILLERS (1969–1971) finished the 13th edition of his legal ethics casebook, Regulation of Lawyers: Problems of Law and Ethics (Aspen), to be published in March 2024. He wrote a law review article identifying how the Department of Justice can be protected from an abusive president, Because They Are Lawyers First and Foremost: “Ethics, Rules and Other Strategies to Protect the Justice Department From a Faithless President,” 57 Georgia Law Review 163 (2022). Stephen became emeritus at New York University Law School and continues to teach professional responsibility there each semester. For the last 25 years, he’s been giving five legal ethics CLE lectures yearly at the NYC Bar Association.
