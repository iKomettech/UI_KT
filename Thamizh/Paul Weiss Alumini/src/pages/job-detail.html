Core Responsibilities

The successful candidate must possess strong knowledge of global trademark law, including clearance, prosecution, enforcement, and contractual work. The candidate must also have experience in adjacent areas including copyright, unfair competition, and social media. Key additional responsibilities include:

  • Collaborating with marketing colleagues to develop global, regional, and local trademark/brand strategy based on business objectives
  • Advising and ensuring that internal clients receive sound, practical, and timely legal advice on matters related to trademarks, copyrights, and social media
  • Reviewing and analyzing search results from jurisdictions all over the world, and conveying results of such analyses to the business in a clear way
  • Preparing trademark applications and other prosecution documents for filing; monitoring and maintaining applications through registration
  • Managing trademark oppositions, cancellations, and litigation around the world; managing outside counsel in those actions
  • Resolving trademark disputes using negotiation skills, judgment, creativity, and legal expertise, including pre-litigation dispute resolution and settlements
  • Reviewing and analyzing trademark watch notices to assess threat to Abbott businesses and develop strategy to combat confusingly similar applications
  • Reviewing and revising intellectual property provisions in agreements
  • Helping select and/or directing the work of outside counsel, defining project objectives, managing projects, and monitoring outside counsel operations within budget.

Minimum Qualifications

  • Ability to work in a highly matrixed and geographically diverse business model including flexibility to work with different time zones where appropriate.
  • Ability to build collaborative relationships with leaders and co-workers at all levels.
  • Ability to travel domestically and internationally when appropriate (approximately 10%).

Critical Success

  • Good Judgment: Demonstrate expertise and sound judgment, understand how assigned responsibilities support Abbott's business objectives, and be able to provide practical, risk-balanced advice based upon a thorough understanding of the scope of an issue, taking full advantage of internal networks and external intelligence.
  • Collaboration: Firm understanding of available legal, functional, and business resources and ability to integrate information from a number of sources to achieve business results, including breaking down boundaries to create alignment as necessary, in a complex, global, matrixed environment. Able to work as part of a team; successful leadership and support of others on the team.

  • Influencing Skills: Build strong relationships with business partners to tactfully influence and gain their support to drive desired outcomes. Be invested in the business you support.
