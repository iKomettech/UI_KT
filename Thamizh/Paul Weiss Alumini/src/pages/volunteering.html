Volunteering

Paul, Weiss’s storied history of pro bono representation and deep commitment to volunteering and community engagement are important parts of our culture. We also like to think that these commitments represent our profession at its best.

2018 American Cancer Society's Making Strides Against Breast Cancer Walk in Central Park

The Paul, Weiss Alumni Network aims to help you stay engaged in service even after you have left the firm. Below, we invite you to learn more about – and even reach out to – the organizations we partner with through our Paul, Weiss Cares initiative. Whether you supported these organizations as a Paul, Weiss attorney or you are learning about them for the first time, each welcomes your interest.

  • AIDS Walk New York has inspired nearly 890,000 people to walk and millions more to donate, raising nearly $150 million to combat HIV and AIDS. The funds raised at the event sustain prevention, care and advocacy programs for thousands of men, women and families affected by the disease in the New York tri-state area.

  • Big Brothers Big Sisters helps children realize their potential and build their futures. As the nation's largest donor- and volunteer- supported mentoring network, the organization makes meaningful, monitored matches between adult volunteers (“Bigs”) and children (“Littles”), ages five through young adulthood, in communities across the country.

  • Career Gear promotes the economic independence of low-income men. The organization provides financial literacy training, a network of support, professional attire, career development tools, job-readiness and essential life-skills training to help men enter the workforce, stay employed and become role models and mentors to their families and communities.

  • City Harvest is New York City's largest food rescue organization, helping to feed the more than 1.2 million New Yorkers who are struggling to put meals on their tables. The organization’s programs help food-insecure New Yorkers access nutritious food that fits their needs and desires and strengthen the local food system, building a path to a food-secure future for all New Yorkers.

  • Cycle for Survival is a movement to beat rare cancers. Its high-energy team-cycling events provide a tangible way for you to fight back. Every dollar raised funds life-saving rare cancer research led by Memorial Sloan Kettering.

  • The International Refugee Assistant Project (IRAP) is the legal advocacy organization for refugees and displaced people in need of a safe place to call home. The organization works with its clients to identify and navigate pathways to safety through free direct legal representation, systemic advocacy and litigation.

  • Justice Served is an effort led by New York City's legal community to close the meal gap in our great city. By donating funds and volunteering, firms and employees help Food Bank For New York City distribute more meals to neighbors in need.

  • Martha's Table is dedicated to finding solutions to poverty in the short term and the long term. The organization addresses emergency needs with food and clothing programs and breaks the long cycle of poverty with education and family strengthening programs. Opportunities to volunteer include helping children and families at no cost pop-up markets, preparing meals, merchandise clothing donations and assisting teachers and students in education program.

  • New York Cares currently engages 65,000 volunteers in service each year. The organization works with about 1,300 nonprofits and schools, and aims to improve education, meet immediate needs and revitalize public spaces throughout NYC. The organization plans and manages about 1,600 volunteer projects each month.

  • One Sandwich At A Time is a grassroots nonprofit that is creating a global understanding and empathy for the homeless population by bringing together communities to feed the homeless. The organization has fed over 100,000 hungry New Yorkers by constructing, bagging and delivering sandwiches to areas in which they are most needed.

  • Operation Backpack campaigns across the country for the thousands of homeless children who go back to school without the basic supplies they need. Selected campaigns provide school supplies and backpacks to children in need.

  • Operation Gratitude seeks to lift the spirits and meet the evolving needs of the U.S. Military and First Responder communities, and provide volunteer opportunities for Americans to express their appreciation to all who serve our nation. Every year, Operation Gratitude sends 300,000+ individually addressed Care Packages to Soldiers, Sailors, Airmen, Marines, and Coast Guardsmen deployed overseas, to their children left behind, and to First Responders, New Recruits, Veterans, Wounded Heroes and their Care Givers.

  • Read Ahead is a reading-based mentoring program for elementary students that helps student develop social-emotional skills essential for academic and life-long success. Volunteers from our corporate and community partners are matched with a student for the school year to read and spend time together during the students' lunch hour each week.
