PATHS FOR LEGAL ENTRY INTO THE UNITED STATES

visa

Generally, Ukrainian nationals and persons of many other nationalities must apply for a visa to enter into the United States legally. There are two broad categories of visas which suggest the person person's initial intentions either to relocate 1) temporarily --(nonimmigrant visas) or 2) permanently immigrant visas. There are many types of visas in each category. Under U.S. immigration law, there is no visa type for people seeking temporary or permanent relocation as refugees. Refugee resettlement is a different process, completely distinct from the visa application process.

NONIMMIGRANT VISAS

(1) Nonimmigrant visas address various needs related to the purposes of travel, scope of activities, and duration of the temporary stay in the United States. It is important that the traveler/petitioner assess eligibility and the application process carefully. For assistance, consult with an immigration attorney licensed to practice in the United States. For general information on nonimmigrant visa categories, see the Department of State Directory of Visa Categories . Specific information about B2 visitor visas, colloquially known as tourist visas, can be found here. See also Department of State Guidance for Nationals of Ukraine.

PAROLE (HUMANITARIAN PAROLE)

Parole is neither a visa nor refugee status. For people who may otherwise be ineligible to enter the United States (e.g., unable to get a visa), parole is permission to remain in the United States for a temporary period because of a compelling emergency, urgent humanitarian reason, or significant public benefit. Parole requests for these reasons are often called Humanitarian Parole.

(1) Uniting for Ukraine
  • (a) This program is a streamlined effort to provide parole for displaced Ukrainian citizens as well as certain immediate family members who are not citizens of Ukraine. The process is distinct from other parole processes. The Biden Harris Administration encourages eligible individuals to use this program in lieu of the other parole options.
