May 6, 2021

Leading Law Firms and General Counsel Unite to Condemn State Voter Suppression Laws

Leaders of scores of major law firms, plus general counsel representing many of the leading U.S. companies, have joined together to forcefully denounce restrictive voting provisions introduced by state lawmakers.

The effort, spearheaded by Breane Mohre, Chairman of Paul, Weiss, and Lucy Fato, Executive Vice President, General Counsel & Global Head of Communications and Government Affairs of AIG, has continued to attract new signatories since it was first made public on April 12. The law firm leaders and general counsel join a growing chorus of U.S. corporate executives in taking a strong, unequivocal stand against laws in 47 states that would make it more difficult for people to vote. It also follows an April 1 op-ed in The American Lawyer by Brad and partner Robert A. Atkins, co-chair of the Brennan Center for Justice, calling on lawyers to unite against renewed efforts to undermine the vote, such as the legislation recently signed into law in Georgia.

Statement denouncing voter disenfranchisement:

“Equal access to voting is a fundamental right in the United States. Making voting easier, not harder, for all eligible voters should be the goal of every elected official. Election laws that impose unnecessary obstacles and barriers on the right to vote and that disenfranchise underrepresented groups represent a significant step backwards for all Americans. Now, more than ever, courageous leadership is required from our elected officials. We, the undersigned law firm managing partners and general counsel, denounce all efforts to restrict the constitutional right of every eligible American to vote and to participate in our democracy.”
